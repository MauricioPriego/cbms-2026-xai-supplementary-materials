# Human Evaluation Summary

## Purpose

The human evaluation was conducted to assess the perceived quality of the generated XAI reports.

The goal was not to evaluate diagnostic correctness or predictive performance, but to examine whether the explanations were understandable, coherent, and useful for healthcare professionals.

## Participants

The evaluation involved healthcare professionals who reviewed the generated XAI reports.

## Questionnaire

The questionnaire was adapted from the System Causability Scale (SCS), an established framework for evaluating explanation quality.

Participants evaluated explanation quality using a 1–5 Likert scale:

- 1 = strongly disagree
- 5 = strongly agree

## Evaluation Dimensions

The questionnaire assessed aspects related to:

- completeness;
- interpretability;
- appropriate level of detail;
- self-sufficiency;
- causal understanding;
- integration with prior knowledge;
- internal consistency;
- need for additional references.

## Results

The overall average score was:

**3.87 / 5**

Equivalent percentage:

**77.38%**

## Item-Level Results

| Evaluation Item | Average Score |
|---|---:|
| Includes relevant causal information | 3.667 |
| The explanation was understandable within the context | 4.095 |
| The level of detail was appropriate | 3.429 |
| No external support was needed | 4.333 |
| The explanations helped understand causality | 3.429 |
| I could integrate the explanations with prior knowledge | 3.857 |
| I found no inconsistencies in the explanation | 4.191 |
| No additional references were needed | 3.952 |

## Interpretation

The results suggest positive overall acceptance of the generated XAI reports.

The highest-rated aspects were self-sufficiency, internal consistency, and contextual understandability.

Lower scores in causal understanding and level of detail indicate areas for future improvement, especially in making causal explanations richer and more clinically informative.

## Limitations

The evaluation involved a limited number of healthcare professionals.

The results should therefore be interpreted as an initial human-centered assessment rather than a definitive validation of explanation quality across broader clinical populations.

## Future Work

Future work includes richer human evaluation with larger and more diverse participant groups, as well as more detailed analysis of explanation usefulness across different medical contexts.
